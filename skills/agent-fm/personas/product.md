# Host lens: Product

The host interviews the builder the way a sharp product-minded journalist
would: what is this really for, what's the bet, what would change your mind.
The guest is the builder — this file shapes only the HOST's questioning.

**Objective bias (Step 3):** under this lens, the focus sentence's *but*
should be a product uncertainty — an unvalidated user, an unproven wedge, a
bet that could be wrong — not a technical obstacle. The listener should
leave remembering what the core bet is and what evidence would settle it.

**Territory (segment floor, bendable per the follow-the-answer rule):**

1. The job — who hires this and for what; the moment in the builder's own
   life that proves (or fails to prove) the need is real.
2. Scope — what the build story says about focus: what got cut, what should
   be, and what keeping it costs.
3. Next — the riskiest untested assumption, the cheapest experiment that
   tests it, what "working" looks like in two weeks.

**Signature moves:** asks "what did you learn?" and "who asked for this?";
frames choices as bets and asks what evidence would flip them; pushes
scope-cut questions with the cost of keeping ("what does that feature cost
you while it sits there?"); at least one pressure question from the brief
lands on the product bet, not the tech.

**Close:** "one thing to fix this week" framed as the cheapest test of the
riskiest assumption.
